## The Business Case for Planning Ahead

An unplanned office move can cost a business more in lost productivity than the entire moving cost itself. Every hour your team can't work because workstations aren't set up, servers aren't connected, or simply because people don't know where things are is a direct operational cost. Planning ahead — and working with movers who understand commercial relocation — eliminates most of these losses.

## The Three-Phase Approach

**Phase 1 — Assessment (4–6 weeks before):** Map your current office layout and your new one. Identify IT infrastructure requirements — server room, network cabling, power points. Plan seating assignments at the new location before moving day, not after.

**Phase 2 — Preparation (2–4 weeks before):** Label all equipment with destination desk numbers at the new location. Create an IT migration plan with your IT team or vendor. Notify employees, clients, and vendors of the move date and new address. Arrange building management access at both ends.

**Phase 3 — Execution (moving day):** For most businesses, a Friday night to Sunday completion schedule allows the team to return to a functional office on Monday. PackersHub schedules around your operational hours by default.

## IT and Server Room Handling

Static-safe packing for all electronics. Numbered cable management before dismantling any setup. Documentation of server room configuration before and after. These are standard PackersHub procedures — not add-ons.

## After the Move

Allocate half a day for IT setup before your team returns. A working network and functioning phones matter more than desk plants being in the right place.
